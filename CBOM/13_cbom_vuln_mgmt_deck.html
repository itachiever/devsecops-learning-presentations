What is Vulnerability Management?

📖 Vulnerability Management is the continuous process of identifying, classifying, prioritising, and remediating security weaknesses in software, systems, and infrastructure. It is not a one-time audit — it is an ongoing security discipline that ensures known weaknesses are found and fixed faster than attackers can exploit them.

🔄 Vulnerability Management Lifecycle — A Continuous Process

1. Discover

Inventory all software, dependencies, and components in every environment — the foundation.

🎯 Where CBOM Fits in VM
  • 1️⃣
    Discover stage — CBOM provides the cryptographic component inventory that VM tools use as input. Without CBOM, crypto components are invisible to traditional VM scanners.
  • 2️⃣
    Identify stage — CBOM's structured fields (name, version, PURL) enable precise CVE matching against NVD, GitHub Advisories, and OSS databases with zero ambiguity.
  • 3️⃣
    Monitor stage — CBOM regenerates on every build, so when a new CVE is published for an OpenSSL version you use, you know within hours — not weeks.
📊 CVSS Severity Scale — How Vulnerabilities Are Scored
0.0–3.9 Low 4.0–6.9 Medium 7.0–8.9 High 9.0–10.0 Critical

💡 Why CVSS matters: A CVE scored CVSS 9.8 (like Log4Shell) means near-certain remote exploitation. A CVSS 4.2 may require local access. CBOM feeds these scores into your risk dashboard so teams always fix the most dangerous issues first.

🐛 CVE Mapping · Slide 3 of 6

What is CVE Mapping?

📖 CVE (Common Vulnerabilities and Exposures) is the global standard for naming and identifying security vulnerabilities. Every publicly known vulnerability receives a unique CVE-ID (e.g., CVE-2021-44228). CVE Mapping is the process of matching your software components — identified by CBOM — against the CVE database to discover which known vulnerabilities affect you specifically.

⚡ How CBOM Enables Precise CVE Matching
  • 🎯
    CBOM stores the exact component PURL (Package URL) — e.g., pkg:generic/openssl@3.0.5 — enabling zero-ambiguity database lookups.
  • 🔗
    CVE scanners match CBOM's component version against affected version ranges in each CVE record — catching exact and partial-version matches.
  • 📡
    When a new CVE is published, the CBOM is automatically re-queried — alerting owners of affected services within minutes, not weeks.

📊 Risk Management · Slide 5 of 6

Risk Management & Prioritisation

📖 With tens or hundreds of CVEs found across a large system, you cannot fix everything at once. Risk management is the art of focusing limited security resources on the vulnerabilities that pose the greatest actual danger. CBOM's usage and dependency data adds crucial business context on top of raw CVSS scores.

🎯 Risk Prioritisation — Focus on What Matters Most First
🔴

Fix Immediately

CVSS ≥ 9.0 + publicly exploited. Active threat. Drop everything and patch within 24 hours.

e.g. Log4Shell, Heartbleed
🟠

Fix This Sprint

CVSS 7.0–8.9 OR exploited in wild. Schedule for current sprint. Assign owner immediately.

e.g. CVE-2022-3602 OpenSSL
🟡

Fix Next Sprint

CVSS 4.0–6.9. Medium risk. Include in next release cycle with standard testing process.

e.g. timing attack vectors
🟢

Monitor & Backlog

CVSS 0–3.9. Low risk. Acknowledge, document, and address in routine maintenance cycle.

e.g. informational findings
