What is Cryptographic Asset Management?

📖 Cryptographic Asset Management (CAM) is the discipline of systematically discovering, cataloguing, monitoring, and controlling all cryptographic components used across an organization's software portfolio. Just as IT asset management tracks servers and software licenses, CAM tracks algorithms, keys, and certificates as formal, governed assets with defined owners, lifecycles, and risk profiles.

⚙️

Algorithm Assets

📌
What it is: The mathematical operations that encrypt, hash, or sign data — AES, RSA, SHA-256, ECDSA.
🎯
Why managed: Algorithms have defined lifespans — MD5 and SHA-1 are retired. New algorithms will be needed for post-quantum security.
📊
Asset attributes: Name · version · strength classification · deprecation status · approved-list membership
🗝️

Key Assets

📌
What it is: Secret values that control encryption and decryption — symmetric keys, RSA public/private pairs, ECDH ephemeral keys.
🎯
Why managed: Keys have defined rotation schedules, storage requirements, and access controls. A leaked key compromises all data it protected.
📊
Asset attributes: Type · bit-length · storage location · owner · last-rotation date · rotation policy
📜

Certificate Assets

📌
What it is: Digital identity documents binding a public key to a domain or service — TLS/SSL certificates, code-signing certs, CA certificates.
🎯
Why managed: Certificates expire on a fixed date — an unmanaged expiry causes immediate production outage and user-facing security warnings.
📊
Asset attributes: Issuer · subject · expiry date · signature algorithm · revocation status · renewal owner

📋 Governance · Slide 3 of 6

What is Governance in Cryptographic Security?

📖 Cryptographic governance is the structured framework of policies, standards, controls, and accountability mechanisms that determine how cryptography is selected, deployed, monitored, and retired across an organization. Without governance, even a well-inventoried CBOM becomes a list — not a control mechanism.

🏛️ Governance Hierarchy — Policy to Operations
🏛️

Crypto Policy

Board / CISO level. Defines organizational commitment: "We will use only NIST-approved algorithms. All cryptographic assets must be inventoried in CBOM." Sets the intent.

📋

Crypto Standards

Security architecture level. Approved algorithm list: AES-256-GCM, RSA-2048+, SHA-256+, TLS 1.2 minimum. Minimum key lengths and required storage mechanisms defined.

⚙️

Crypto Controls

Engineering level. CI/CD policy gates, CBOM mandatory fields, automated CVE alerts, certificate renewal SLAs, key rotation enforcement — operationalizing the standards.

🔍

Monitoring & Audit

Security operations level. Continuous CBOM scanning, dashboard reporting, exception tracking, quarterly risk reviews submitted to CISO and compliance teams.

📊

Reporting & Accountability

Governance reporting level. Named CBOM owners, SLA breach escalations, regulatory submissions (CERT-In, RBI, SEBI), executive risk dashboards.

📜 Governance Policy Examples
📋

Approved Algorithm Policy

Defines which algorithms are permitted for each use case. Symmetric encryption: AES-256-GCM only. Hashing: SHA-256 or SHA-3. No MD5, SHA-1, or DES for any security function.
Enforced by: CBOM CI/CD gate blocking non-compliant builds automatically
🗝️

Key Management Policy

Minimum RSA-2048 or ECC-256. All production keys stored in HSM or approved KMS (AWS KMS, HashiCorp Vault). Rotation every 90 days. No hardcoded keys in source code.
Enforced by: CBOM key-storage field checked against approved-storage list on each scan
📜

Certificate Lifecycle Policy

All certs must use SHA-256 signature algorithm. Maximum 1-year validity. Renewal initiated 60 days before expiry. OCSP stapling required for all public-facing TLS.
Enforced by: CBOM certificate expiry alerts at 90d/30d/7d with escalating severity
🔗

Protocol Version Policy

TLS 1.3 required for all new services. TLS 1.2 acceptable with approved cipher suites. TLS 1.0/1.1 and SSLv3 strictly prohibited. SSH-2 only for all remote access.
Enforced by: CBOM runtime protocol scan flags violations in real time
